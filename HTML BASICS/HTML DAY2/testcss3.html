Educational Qualification

Scientists once rejected the idea that cats, like animals in general, have personalities. Over time however, it has been recognised that it is important to be able to recognise and assess the personalities of cats, because this can result in better keeping and care of the animal. If both are adapted to the character of the cat, it ensures or improves both the well-being and the health of the cat and increases its quality of life. Feline five As part of a study, researchers have defined five main personality traits of cats ('Feline Five'), each of which summarises a large number of related traits. The research team grouped them under the terms neuroticism, extraversion, dominance, impulsivity and tolerance. Each of these characteristics can be developed to different extents. Every cat has all of these characteristics, but in different markedness. When evaluating your own cat, the extremes are interesting and important.
